Understanding Electrocardiograms

Learn about ECG technology and its critical role in cardiac diagnosis

What is an ECG?

An electrocardiogram (ECG) is a medical test that records the electrical activity of the heart over time. It captures the heart's rhythm and can detect various cardiac conditions through characteristic patterns in the electrical signals.

12-Lead Analysis

Our system analyzes standard 12-lead ECG recordings, which provide a comprehensive view of the heart's electrical activity from multiple angles, enabling accurate detection of various cardiac abnormalities.

AI-Powered Detection

Advanced machine learning algorithms trained on thousands of ECG recordings provide rapid, accurate classification of cardiac conditions, assisting healthcare professionals in diagnosis and treatment planning.

Detectable Cardiac Conditions

Our AI system can identify and classify 10 different cardiac conditions with high accuracy

NORM

Normal ECG

Healthy cardiac electrical activity with normal rhythm, rate, and waveform patterns.

MI

Myocardial Infarction

Heart attack - damage to heart muscle due to blocked blood supply, requiring immediate medical attention.

CD

Conduction Disturbance

Abnormal electrical conduction pathways affecting the heart's rhythm and coordination.

HYP

Hypertrophy

Enlarged heart muscle, often due to high blood pressure or other cardiovascular conditions.

STTC

ST/T Changes

Abnormal ST segment or T wave patterns indicating potential ischemia or other cardiac issues.

LVH

Left Ventricular Hypertrophy

Thickening of the left ventricle wall, often associated with hypertension or valve disease.

LAFB

Left Anterior Fascicular Block

Blocked electrical pathway in the left ventricle affecting the heart's conduction system.

ISC

Ischemic

Reduced blood flow to heart muscle, potentially indicating coronary artery disease.

IRBBB

Incomplete Right Bundle Branch Block

Partial blockage in the right bundle branch affecting electrical conduction.

IVCD

Intraventricular Conduction Disturbance

Abnormal electrical conduction within the ventricles affecting heart rhythm coordination.

Help & Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about using the ECG classification system

What file formats are supported?

The system supports WFDB format files, which consist of paired .dat (binary data) and .hea (header) files. Both files are required for analysis.

How accurate are the AI predictions?

Our AI model has been trained on thousands of ECG recordings and provides high accuracy classifications. However, results should always be reviewed by qualified medical professionals for final diagnosis.

Is patient data stored on the system?

No, we prioritize patient privacy. ECG files are processed temporarily and automatically deleted after analysis. No patient data is permanently stored on our servers.

Can I export the analysis results?

Currently, results are displayed on-screen. Future updates will include export functionality for PDF reports and CSV data files.
